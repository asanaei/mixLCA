# Synthetic Health-Screening Dataset with Distal Outcome

A simulated dataset of 800 subjects undergoing a health screening
protocol. Four continuous biomarkers form a two-class latent structure;
an antecedent age covariate shifts class membership; a binary screening
outcome (`yes` / `no`) follows the latent class with a mild additional
age effect.

## Usage

``` r
health_screening
```

## Format

A data frame with 800 rows and 6 columns.

- marker_1:

  Numeric. Loosely log-normal continuous indicator (class-1 median
  around 80, class-2 around 140).

- marker_2:

  Numeric. Loosely log-normal continuous indicator (class-1 median
  around 70, class-2 around 95).

- marker_3:

  Numeric. Loosely log-normal continuous indicator (class-1 median
  around 22, class-2 around 33).

- marker_4:

  Numeric. Loosely log-normal continuous indicator (class-1 median
  around 0.30, class-2 around 0.60).

- age:

  Numeric. Age in years (truncated to 25-70).

- outcome:

  Factor with levels `no` and `yes`: the binary distal outcome.

## Source

Synthetic data, generated by `data-raw/make-example-data.R`.

## Details

This dataset is designed to demonstrate the partitioned architecture
supported by
[`fit_lca`](https://asanaei.github.io/mixLCA/reference/fit_lca.md):
concomitant predictors (here `age`) enter the measurement model through
multinomial logistic regression on class membership, while the distal
outcome (`outcome`) is estimated separately via
[`distal`](https://asanaei.github.io/mixLCA/reference/distal.md) under
Bolck-Croon-Hagenaars (BCH) inverse- classification-error weighting.

The data are synthetic, authored by the package author for didactic
purposes, and are licensed under GPL (\>= 3) along with the rest of the
package. A reproducible generation script is provided in the source tree
at `data-raw/make-example-data.R`.

We deliberately do not derive this dataset from any real screening
cohort. Labeling participants by latent "metabolic class" can carry
substantive implications that we leave to domain researchers; the
synthetic construction here avoids importing those framings while
preserving the joint structure (mixed continuous indicators, antecedent
covariate, distal outcome) that the package is designed to model.

## References

For methodological background on the partitioned LCA architecture used
here, see Vermunt (2010), *Latent class modeling with covariates: two
improved three-step approaches*, Political Analysis, 18(4), 450-469.
