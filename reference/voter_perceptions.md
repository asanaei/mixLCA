# Synthetic Voter Perceptions of Two Hypothetical Candidates

A simulated dataset of 2000 respondents rating two hypothetical
political candidates ("A" and "B") on six attributes: moral character
(`moral`), competence (`compet`), leadership (`lead`), honesty
(`honest`), intelligence (`intel`), and empathy (`empath`). Each
attribute appears twice, once for each candidate, distinguished by the
suffix `_A` or `_B`.

## Usage

``` r
voter_perceptions
```

## Format

A data frame with 2000 rows and 12 columns. All columns are ordered
factors with levels `poor < fair < good < excellent`.

- moral_A, moral_B:

  Rating of moral character for candidates A and B.

- compet_A, compet_B:

  Rating of competence.

- lead_A, lead_B:

  Rating of leadership.

- honest_A, honest_B:

  Rating of honesty.

- intel_A, intel_B:

  Rating of intelligence.

- empath_A, empath_B:

  Rating of empathy.

## Source

Synthetic data, generated by `data-raw/make-example-data.R`.

## Details

Ratings are ordered four-level factors (`poor`, `fair`, `good`,
`excellent`). The generative model is a three-class mixture: a strong
A-favoring class (40%), a strong B-favoring class (40%), and a moderate
/ ambivalent class (20%). Within each class, a shared latent factor
induces local dependence between the six items rating the same
candidate; this makes the dataset useful for demonstrating bivariate
residual diagnostics
([`bvr_categorical`](https://asanaei.github.io/mixLCA/reference/bvr_categorical.md))
and spectral local dependence
([`auto_sld`](https://asanaei.github.io/mixLCA/reference/auto_sld.md)).

The data are synthetic, authored by the package author for didactic
purposes, and are licensed under GPL (\>= 3) along with the rest of the
package. A reproducible generation script is provided in the source tree
at `data-raw/make-example-data.R`.

The substantive analog (not the source) is the rating-scale design used
in the American National Election Studies (ANES), where respondents rate
presidential candidates on attribute dimensions. For published analyses
of real ANES data, see for example Bartle (2000) or related
political-cognition literature.

## References

Bartle, J. (2000). Political awareness, opinion constraint and the
stability of ideological positions. *Political Studies*, 48(3), 467-484.

## Examples

``` r
data(voter_perceptions)
head(voter_perceptions)
#>     moral_A   moral_B  compet_A  compet_B    lead_A    lead_B  honest_A
#> 1 excellent      fair excellent excellent      good excellent excellent
#> 2      fair excellent      fair      good      poor      fair      poor
#> 3 excellent      poor excellent      fair excellent      fair      poor
#> 4 excellent      poor      good      poor excellent excellent excellent
#> 5      fair      poor      good      poor excellent      poor excellent
#> 6      fair      poor      poor      poor      poor      fair      poor
#>    honest_B   intel_A   intel_B  empath_A  empath_B
#> 1      good excellent      good excellent      poor
#> 2 excellent      poor excellent      poor excellent
#> 3 excellent excellent      poor excellent      good
#> 4      poor excellent      fair      good      fair
#> 5      poor      good      poor excellent      poor
#> 6      poor      poor      poor      poor      poor
table(voter_perceptions$moral_A, voter_perceptions$moral_B)
#>            
#>             poor fair good excellent
#>   poor       185  154  166       230
#>   fair       147   77   77       102
#>   good       169   69   62        85
#>   excellent  207   94   88        88
```
